# Information Security Policy – Cyzilla Ltd  
**Version 1.0 – 01 April 2025 – Approved by: Managing Director**

### Purpose
Cyzilla Ltd is committed to preserving the **confidentiality, integrity and availability** of all information assets entrusted to us by our clients and required to run our business.

As a cyber risk consultancy handling sensitive client data (including OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE material) information security is central to our reputation and legal obligations.

### Policy Statement
Top management commits to:
1. Establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS) in conformity with **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**
2. Satisfying all applicable requirements including:
   - UK GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018
   - Contractual and NDA obligations with clients and cyber insurers
   - NCSC Cyber Essentials Plus (achieved March 2025)
3. Setting and reviewing information security objectives at least annually
4. Providing adequate resources and competence for the ISMS
5. Ensuring risks are identified, assessed and treated to an acceptable level
6. Promoting a positive security culture through leadership and example

### Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors and third parties who have access to Cyzilla information assets (see Scope Statement).

### Key Principles
- Information will be classified and protected according to its sensitivity
- Access granted on least-privilege and need-to-know basis
- All breaches and suspected breaches must be reported immediately
- All staff complete annual security awareness training
- The ISMS is subject to internal audit and management review

### Responsibilities
- **Managing Director (CISO)** – Ultimate accountability for information security
- **All staff** – Day-to-day responsibility for protecting information

This policy is reviewed annually or after any significant incident/change.
