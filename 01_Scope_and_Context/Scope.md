# ISMS Scope Statement – CypherRisk Ltd  
**Version 1.0 – 01 April 2025 – Approved by: Managing Director**

### Scope of the Information Security Management System (Clause 4.3)

The scope of the ISIS covers **all information security processes and assets required to deliver cyber risk consulting services** to UK clients, including:

**In scope**
- All information processed, stored or transmitted within the Microsoft Azure (UK South) subscription and Microsoft 365 E5 tenant (UK data residency)
- All five company-owned, Intune-managed Windows laptops used by the consultants
- Customer data (client risk assessments, penetration test reports, board papers) classified up to OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE
- Internal business information (contracts, financial data, methodology playbooks)
- All employees and contractors who have access to the above

**Locations**
- Fully remote workforce (home offices) + occasional use of Regus Cheltenham for client meetings (no fixed office)

**Exclusions and justification**
- Physical office premises → None owned or leased (co-working only – excluded under A.7 controls)
- On-premise servers → None exist (cloud-only)

**Boundaries and applicability**
The ISMS applies to all business processes supporting the delivery of:
- ISO 27001 & NIS gap assessments
- NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF) reviews
- Cyber insurance underwriting support
- Penetration testing scoping and reporting

This scope has been determined considering:
- Internal and external issues (Clause 4.1) – small remote team, high-value sensitive client data, FCA regulatory oversight
- Interested parties (Clause 4.2) – clients, FCA principal firm, cyber insurers, Microsoft (cloud provider)
- Interfaces and dependencies – full dependency on Microsoft shared responsibility model
